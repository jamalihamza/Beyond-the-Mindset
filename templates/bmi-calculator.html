What is BMI?

BMI, or Body Mass Index, is a measurement that uses your weight and height to determine whether you're underweight, at a healthy weight, overweight, or obese. It serves as a quick reference for understanding your overall health status and how your body weight compares to standard ranges. While it provides a good overview, it’s important to remember that BMI doesn’t account for muscle mass or fat distribution and should be used alongside other health assessments.

How to Calculate BMI?

BMI is calculated by dividing your weight (in kilograms) by your height (in meters squared). The resulting number fits into different ranges, each indicating a general health status:

  • Below 18.5: Underweight
  • 18.5 - 24.9: Healthy weight
  • 25 - 29.9: Overweight
  • 30 or above: Obese

For children and teens, BMI also considers age and gender, as growth patterns vary widely.

Why is BMI Important?

Tracking your BMI can help you stay on top of your fitness journey. Whether you're aiming to lose weight, build muscle, or maintain a healthy balance, knowing your BMI is a good starting point for setting realistic goals. It can also serve as a health indicator, with a normal BMI range associated with lower risks of heart disease, diabetes, and other weight-related conditions.

However, for more precise measurements, especially for individuals with higher muscle mass or special health conditions, it’s important to use other tools, such as body fat percentage or waist circumference.
